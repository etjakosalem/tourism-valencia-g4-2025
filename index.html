About Valencia

Nestled in the heart of Negros Oriental, Valencia is a charming town that offers the perfect blend of nature, culture, and adventure. Known for its lush landscapes, rolling hills, and cool climate, Valencia is a haven for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

This town is a gateway to some of Negros Oriental’s most scenic spots, from pristine rivers and waterfalls to hidden mountain trails. Valencia’s rich history and warm, welcoming community add a cultural depth to every visit, making it not just a destination, but an experience.

Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural immersion, or simply a peaceful retreat surrounded by nature, Valencia promises an unforgettable journey for every traveler.

Food

Unique food offerings of Valencia, where traditional flavors and local favorites come together.

Valechon

Valencia’s signature twist on the classic lechon—crisp, flavorful, and slow-roasted to perfection. Valechon is known for its juicy meat, perfectly seasoned skin, and rich aroma that draws locals and visitors alike.

Pungko Pungko

A fun and budget-friendly street-food experience where you sit on low stools and enjoy a spread of fried favorites—lumpia, pork, chicken, eggs, and more. Simple, tasty, and very local, it’s a must-try food stop for anyone craving authentic Valencia flavors in a casual setting.

Ihaw-Ihaw

A classic Filipino grilling experience featuring skewered meats, seafood, and local favorites cooked over hot charcoal. Smoky, savory, and best enjoyed with friends—this is Valencia’s go-to street food for a flavorful night out.

Rambones Festival

The Rambones Festival is celebrated every November and highlights Valencia’s vibrant agricultural heritage, especially its famous rambutan and lansones. The festival features colorful street dancing, lively cultural performances, and a dramatized story of two mythical tribes, making it one of the town’s most energetic and visually stunning celebrations.

Chada Valencia Festival

Held every May, the Chada Valencia Festival showcases the town’s culture, talents, products, and traditions. Throughout the month, locals and visitors enjoy a variety of events such as trade fairs, garden shows, talent competitions, thanksgiving ceremonies, and sports activities. It’s a community-centered celebration that brings Valencia to life.
